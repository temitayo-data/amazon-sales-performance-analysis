## Project Overview

This project analyzes Amazon sales data from 2019 to 2024 to uncover key business insights across revenue trends, product performance, customer behavior, and operational efficiency.

The goal is to identify growth opportunities, evaluate profitability drivers, and highlight critical business challenges such as high return rates and pricing inefficiencies.

---

## Dataset Summary

The dataset contains transactional sales data with the following features:

- Order details (Order ID, Order Date, Order Status)
- Customer data (Customer ID, Customer Name, Region)
- Product data (Category, Product Name)
- Sales metrics (Quantity Sold, Total Sales, Profit Margin)
- Pricing factors (Discount %)
- Payment methods and salesperson data

## Executive Insights

### 1. Peak Performance in 2022 Followed by Decline

Sales and profit both peaked in 2022, reaching their highest levels before experiencing a steady decline through 2024. This suggests a slowdown in demand or operational inefficiencies post-2022.

---

### 2. Stable Monthly Sales with Mid-Year Peak

Sales remain relatively stable across months, with peak performance occurring in May and June. February consistently records the lowest sales, indicating seasonal demand fluctuations.

---

### 3. Core Categories Drive Business Performance

Sports and Clothing categories generate the highest sales and profit, followed by Toys and Electronics. These categories represent the primary revenue drivers of the business.

---

### 4. Weak Categories Contribute Minimal Value

Home & Kitchen, Books, and Beauty categories show low sales and profit, indicating underperformance and potential inefficiencies in product positioning or demand.

---

### 5. Balanced Product Portfolio with No Dominant Product

Top products contribute similar revenue levels, suggesting a diversified portfolio but a lack of strong best-selling products.

---

### 6. Discount Strategy is Category-Dependent

Discounts are effective in high-performing categories (Sports, Clothing, Electronics, Toys), where profitability remains strong.

However, in categories like Books, high discounts lead to low profit margins, indicating pricing inefficiencies.

---

### 7. Critical Operational Issue: High Return Rate

Returned orders (26.5%) exceed completed orders (24.6%), indicating significant operational inefficiencies and potential customer dissatisfaction.

Additionally, cancellations and pending orders account for nearly half of all transactions, further highlighting process challenges.

---

### 8. Returns are a System-Wide Issue

Return rates are consistently high across all categories (25%–29%), with Home & Kitchen and Clothing slightly higher.

This suggests that the issue is not category-specific but rather a systemic problem related to product quality, expectations, or fulfillment.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Improve Order Fulfillment and Reduce Returns
- Enhance product quality and packaging  
- Improve delivery accuracy  
- Align product descriptions with customer expectations  

---

### 2. Optimize Pricing and Discount Strategy
- Apply category-specific discount strategies  
- Reduce excessive discounting in low-margin categories (e.g., Books)  

---

### 3. Focus on High-Performing Categories
- Increase investment in Sports, Clothing, Electronics, and Toys  
- Expand product offerings in these categories  

---

### 4. Reassess Underperforming Categories
- Improve visibility and marketing  
- Bundle products or adjust pricing  
- Reduce focus if ROI remains low  

---

### 5. Investigate 2022 Peak Performance
- Identify key drivers behind peak year performance  
- Replicate successful strategies across future periods  

---

## Conclusion

The analysis reveals a business with strong core product categories and stable sales patterns, but facing critical operational challenges, particularly in order fulfillment and returns.

Addressing these issues while optimizing pricing strategies and focusing on high-performing categories will be key to sustaining long-term growth and profitability.
